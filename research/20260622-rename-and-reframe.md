# 20260622 - Rename and Reframe

## What I did

- Documented the engine/data repo split in the README: added short descriptions of `coach.zander.cfk` (the public engine) and `coach.zander.cfk.data` (the private content), and fixed list formatting that had broken along the way.
- Prefixed both existing research journal entry titles with their date, so the in-file heading matches the `YYYYMMDD-topic.md` filename convention.
- Fixed typos and wording issues in the README.
- Renamed the repository's framing from "From Agile to Agentic" to "Adaptive × Agentic," rewriting the README's core argument: agentic isn't a successor to agile, it's this decade's disruption — alongside digitalization and globalization before it — and being adaptive is what determines whether a business, leadership, and operating model benefits from it instead of being disrupted by it. Restructured "What I am exploring" into three explicit lenses (business model, leadership model, operating model) instead of a flat list of questions.

## Technical Learnings

- Renaming the repo's core framing only four days after the initial commit confirmed that documentation churn is cheap when caught early — much cheaper than realizing the framing was off after several more journal entries had already committed to "Agile to Agentic" language.
- The journal-title mismatch was a small consistency bug: filenames already followed `YYYYMMDD-topic.md`, but the markdown heading inside hadn't caught up. A convention needs to be enforced in more than one place (filename and content) to actually hold.

## Organizational Learnings

- Precise documentation using a repo is helpful when analysing the things that have changed - even by AI.
